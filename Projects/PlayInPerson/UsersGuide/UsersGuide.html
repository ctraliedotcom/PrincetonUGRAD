1. Overview

 

You are about to join a gaming community unlike any before it: an online social gaming network in which players compete not only coast to coast, but also face to face.  PlayInPerson brings people closer together through video-chat based gaming.  Imagine playing distant friends across the country from the confines of your own home as if they were right in front of you.  This breakthrough integration of live video-chat based gaming bridges a glaring chasm between online multiplayer games and the social interactions for which they are meant to substitute.  Now you can play online multiplayer games with your favorite people in person.  PlayInPerson brings back the pokerface in online poker, and introduces a new dimension of heckling in games like Set and Bomberman.  So why play behind the LCD screen when you can play in person?  Give it a try at www.playinperson.com.  Your group of weekend amigos just got a whole lot bigger.

 

 

2. Getting Started

 

Signing In

 

Before the fun begins, you must first sign into PlayInPerson with a unique username.  There are two ways to accomplish this task:  sign in using a personal user account or anonymously using a throwaway guest account.  

 

Creating a PlayInPerson Account

 

User accounts offer a personalized PlayInPerson experience.  As usernames are unique, friends can be sure that it is your username they see on PlayInPerson, and not another user with the same username.  As the website grows, additional features will allow registered users to maintain a buddy list of friends on PlayInPerson, keep a record of their game play performance, as well as having the option to display biographical information in a user profile section.  These features require a personal PlayInPerson user account.  Luckily, creating your PlayInPerson account is easier than tying your shoes.

 

1.        To create your PlayInPerson personal user account, first click the “Register” button on the PlayInPerson login page.

 

2.        Enter your desired username, email address, and password.

 

3.        Click “Submit” and you have just created your own PlayInPerson account!

 

When registering, please enter a valid email address, as in the future, registered users will have the option of receiving email updates regarding PlayInPerson.  Also, make sure you enter the same password in the password confirmation field, to help ensure that you have entered your desired password correctly.

 

Signing into Your PlayInPerson Account

 

1.        Once you have created a PlayInPerson account, sign in by first visiting the main PlayInPerson login page (www.playinperson.com).

 

2.        At the login page, enter your username and password.

 

3.        Click the “Login” button.

 

Some users may simply desire a quick game on PlayInPerson, without taking the time to create a user account.  Other users might may want to maintain more anonymity on PlayInPerson.  These users can sign into PlayInPerson using a throwaway guest account.  Signing in as a guest is simple:

 

Signing in Using as an Anonymous Guest

 

1.        On the main PlayInPerson login page, under “Sign in as a guest,” enter your desired guest username.

 

2.        Press the “Enter” button.

 

Upon signing into PlayInPerson, you will be taken to the main PlayInPerson lobby, a chatroom in which you may send text messages to fellow users and coordinate games.

 

Navigating the Main Lobby

 

The main lobby is where you arrive upon signing into PlayInPerson.  Here you join all other users who are logged in.  Users in the main lobby may communicate by sending textual messages to the chatroom, allowing users to meet fellow gamers and to coordinate games with friends.  Any message a users types and sends in the chatroom will appear in the main chat dialogue prefixed by the username, and will be made visible to all users in the chatroom, among whom will often include a PlayInPerson site moderator.  Such public display of user communication in the main lobby helps prevent hostile or malicious user-user interactions.

 

In the main lobby, the usernames of all logged-in users are displayed in the user list to the right of the main chatroom dialogue box.  The user list is viewable if the “Users” tab at the upper right of the main lobby frame is selected.

 

Game Room Creation

 

To start playing a game with friends on PlayInPerson, first create a game room for the game you wish to play.  Create a new game room by following the steps provided below:

 

1.        Above the main lobby frame, select the “New Game” tab. You will be taken to the game creation page.

 

2.        In the drop-down room size menu, select the room size, which the the number of total players allowed in the game.  Currently this number may vary from 2 to 6 players.

 

3.        In drop-down game menu, select the game you wish to play.

 

 

4.        Click “Create.”  You will be taken to your newly created game room.

 

5.        A pop-up Flash Player Settings dialogue box will notify you that PlayInPerson may use peer-assisted networking, giving you the choice to allow or disallow this feature.  Select “Allow,” which will allow PlayInPerson to utilizes P2P communication among players to send webcam video and microphone audio data, allowing you to interact with friends in real time as you play them in games.

 

 

6.        A second Flash Player Settings dialogue box asks you for permission to access your webcam and microphone.  Select “Allow” if you wish your friends to see and hear you in the game room.  You will subsequently be taken into the game room.

 

Within the game room, player video streams are displayed at the left.  You should be able to see your own webcam video stream displayed among the other empty video boxes.  Each empty video box will display the corresponding video stream of their player’s webcam once that player has entered the gameroom.

Underneath the video boxes is the game room chat box, where players can converse textually.

 

To the right of the video boxes and chat box is the actual game, which you may interact with as you would any web-game.  Players may interact within the game in real time.  For more information on how to play the various games currently offered by PlayInPerson, see the Games section.

 

Now that you have figured out your way around your new game room, you can start inviting friends using the Invitation System detailed in the following section.

 

Note that if more players are invited than the corresponding game accomodates, excess users act as spectators.  Further note that presently, PlayInPerson allows users to be the creator of at most one game room at any time.  To create a new game room, first exit any active game rooms you have already created.

 

To leave the game room, click the “Leave” button at the upper-left of the game room page.

 

 

The Invitation System

 

This sections details proper usage of the PlayInPerson invitation system, which allows you to invite other users to join game rooms in which you are active, and to accept game room invitations from fellow users.

 

Inviting Users to a Game Room

 

1.  Select the “Chat” tab above the game room frame to view the Main Lobby.

 

2.  Hover your mouse cursor over the name of the user in the user list whom you would like to invite into the game room.

 

3.  Click the “Invite” button appears next to the selected user’s username to send the selected user your invitation.

 

4.  To return to the game room, select the tab above the Main Lobby frame corresponding to the name of the game being played in the game room.

 

5.  Once the invited user accepts your invitation, the invited user will be taken to the game room, where the user’s video stream will be visible.  At this point you and the invited user may begin game play or invite additional users to join the game room.

 

Accepting a Game Room Invitation

 

1.  When another user invites you to a game room, a dialogue box will appear notifying you of this invitation, and asking you to accept the invitation.

2.  Accepting the invitation takes you to the corresponding game room.

 

3.  Deferred invitations become queued in the “Invites” list, viewable by selecting the “Invites” tab next to the ”Users” tab to the right of the chat dialogue of the Main Lobby.

 

4.  Selecting the “Invites” tab displays the list of deferred invites in place of the Main Lobby user list.  

 

5.  To accept a deferred invitation, click the “Accept” button next to the listed invitation.  You will be taken to the corresponding game room if that game room is still active.  If the game room is no longer active, you the deferred invitation will be removed from your deferred invites list.

 

The Video Chat Interface

 

The video streams at the left of the game room display the live video and audio streams of the other players in the game.  The output settings of each video stream is easily adjusted.

 

Adjusting Audio Stream Volume

 

1.  To adjust the volume of each audio stream, first hover your mouse cursor over the corresponding video box.

2.  A vertical slide menu will appear, allowing you to select a new volume between 0.0 and 100.0, where greater values indicate louder volumes.

 

Adjusting Video Stream Settings

 

1.  To adjust your personal video stream settings, hover your mouse cursor over the video box corresponding to your own video stream.

2.  Click the icon of two gears that will appear at the upper-right of your video box.

3.  Your video stream settings menu will appear, where you can select the appropriate microphone and webcam inputs and alter your microphone volume on a slide menu (loudness increasing rightwards).

 

3. Games

 

Tic-Tac-Toe (2 players)

 

Tic-Tac-Toe is a simple game for two players: X and O.  Turns alternate between the two players.  When it is a player’s turn, the player may place their symbol (which is either X or O) on one of the 9 squares in the game grid currently without a symbol.  The first player to successfully create a horizontal, vertical, or diagonal line of three of their corresponding symbols wins the game.  If no player succeeds at this task by the time all 9 squares have been filled, the game ends in a tie.

 

Set (Up to 6 players)

 

Set is an addictive card-based puzzle game.  PlayInPerson currently allows for up to 6 players to engage in a game of Set.

 

In Set, there is a deck of 81 cards.  Each card has between 1 to 3 copies of the same symbol.  There are 27 symbols, satisfying all possible combinations of shape, color, and fill, each attribute having 3 possible values.  Treating the number of symbols as its own attribute, each card may be defined by a unique combination of values of four attributes:

 

Number: 1, 2, or 3.

Shape: Oval, stadium, or squiggle.

Color: Red, green, or blue.

Fill: Empty, filled, or lines.

 

Each of the four attributes takes on 3 possible values, resulting in 3^4 Set cards.

 

The objective of Set is to find a sets of 3 cards such that among the three cards, each attribute is either all the same or all different.  For example the following 3 cards comprise a valid set because among all 3 cards, the shape and numbers are the same while the number and fill are all different.

 

The Game of Set begins with a randomly shuffled deck and 21 cards from the deck in play, which is provably the minimum number required to guarantee the presence of a valid set.

 

A player selects sets of 3 cards by clicking the corresponding cards.  If the selected 3 cards indeed comprise a valid set, the 3 cards are removed from play and replaced by cards from the top of the deck if there are cards left in the deck.  The player gains 3 points for each valid set found.  A game of Set ends when the deck runs out of cards, and all remaining valid sets have been found.  The player who finds the most sets and hence has the highest score by the time the deck runs out of cards wins the game.

 

Once the game ends, to begin a new game, click the icon of the Set deck to reshuffle the deck and deal out 21 new cards.  The game then commences as before.

 

Texas Hold’em Poker (2 to 6 players)

 

Texas Hold’em Poker is a traditional casino card game in which players place bets against each other’s dealt hands.  PlayInPerson currently allows for up to 6 players to engage in a game of Texas Hold’em.  

 

The player who creates the room is the game administrator, responsible for controlling the game flow (deciding when to start the game once enough people have joined the game room, and when to reset the game).  The administrator has access to his or her administrative options via the drop-down menu at the right of the game display.  

 

Before a game of Texas Hold’em starts, each player selects a seat around the card table in the game interface.  A player may also choose to stand and spectate the game at any time by clicking the button corresponding to the seat around the card table.  Even though there are only 6 spots for actual players, an unlimited amount of spectators can exist.  Once everyone is seated in the spots they want, the game administrator can start the game.  Users can still sit and stand once the game has started, and this is dealt with gracefully, but we expect most people not to do this as often as they would before the game starts.

 

Initially, each player starts with $5000 in poker chips.  At the beginning of each round of betting, each player is dealt two cards from the shuffled deck.  A round of betting follows, in which players put money into the community “pot” in the center of the table.  In each round, the game cycles through all players and allows them to take action.  When it’s a player’s turn, he/she can fold (leave the round and sacrifice any money he/she has bet), call (put in money to match the current bid in the pot), check (do nothing in the event that the money they’ve put in matches the current bit already), raise (put in more money to face other players to call), or go all in (put in all of their money into the pot).  In subsequent stages of betting in a round, additional cards will be dealt onto the card table that are visible to all players.  A player must create the best hand possible of at most 5 cards out of his or her two initially dealt cards and the cards on the card table.  For each pot, there are four rounds of betting (in which all players are cycled through until the players left in the game all match the money in the pot).  After all rounds of betting, the player with the best hand wins the pot.

 

Here are a few more specific details about how betting works.  In each round of betting, turns proceed clockwise from the designated “Big Blind” player, who is required to place an amount of chips equal to the blind ($100) into that round’s pot (amount of chips the winner receives).  The “Small Blind” player directly before the “Big Blind” player must place half of the blind ($50) into the pot.  The blinds act as the initial bet for the round.  At each player’s turn, he or she must first call the current bet for that round, and has the option to raise the current bet.  After every player has placed his or her bets, the players who have not matched the current bet are given a chance to call the bet or to fold their hand.  Betting continues without new cards being turned over as long as people continue to raise.  Note also that the “Big Blind” and “Small Blind” player shift one player clockwise with each subsequent pot.

 

After this initially round of betting, a set of 3 cards called “the flop” is dealt onto the table from the top of the deck, then an additional card called “the turn” is dealt, followed by a final card from the top of the deck called “the river.”  A round of betting follows immediately after “the flop,” “the turn,” and the “river.”  The same rules of betting follow after each of the flop, the turn, and the river.

Once the final betting has completed, players turn over their cards and a winner is determined.  The winner usually takes all of the money in the pot, except in some special cases that result in a “split pot” between several players (the winner went all in and only gets the money that matched that bet, or there were ties).  These rules are a bit complicated and not worth delving into too much detail here, but the game deals with these cases correctly as far as we know.

At the end of each round, the players who are still in the game must confirm that they are ready to move onto the next round and to be dealt new cards by clicking the “ready for next round” button.  This is to give players a chance to view the cards that opponents have to understand why a certain player won, before the cards get put away and reshuffled.

 

A player loses Texas Hold’em once he or she has lost all of his or her chips, but may sit down at another spot that still has chips left.  The game is over as soon as there’s only one person sitting who still has money, with said person winning the game.

One more thing to note is that each player can only see his/her two cards, except in some special circumstances, such as when a player goes all in and has no more money to put in (he/she has to show his/her cards as soon as the betting comes back around and he/she doesn’t have enough money to call the current bid value).  A player is not required to show his/her cards if he/she is the only one left in the round and everyone else folds.  But players who stay in a round must always show their cards at the end of a round to determine who is the winner.

 

The list of possible card hands in Texas Hold’em in order of descending value is provided below:

 

Royal Flush: ten, jack, queen, king, and ace all of the same suit.

Straight Flush: five cards in sequence of the same suit.

Four of a Kind: four cards of the same denomination.

Full House: three of a kind and a pair.

Flush: any five cards of the same suit.

Straight: five cards in sequence of any suit (note that an ace can count as a 1 in order to make this happen).

Three of a Kind: three cards of the same denomination.

Two Pairs: two sets of two cards of the same denomination.

Pair: a pair of cards of the same denomination.

High Card:  in the absence of any hand listed above, the card of the highest denomination.

 

Bomberman (Up to 4 players)

 

In the game of Bomberman, users control animated sprites that may move around a grid space filled with block obstacles using the arrow keys of the keyboard.

 

Players may command their sprites to drop “bombs” at the sprite’s current location by pressing the space bar.

 

Dropped bombs detonate after 5 seconds, and any other player whose sprite is within the blast radius of the bomb will be “killed,” including the player who dropped the bomb.

 

The explosion emitted by a bomb cannot travel past the block obstacles.  Hiding behind a block obstacle is an effective means to evade a bomb blast, the other alternative being to run away quickly.

 

Upon dropping a bomb, player gains 1 point for every other player eliminated by the bomb.  If the player is accidentally eliminated by his or her own bomb, he or she loses 1 point.

 

Once eliminated by a bomb, a player may rejuvenate their sprite, and return to the game by pressing space, which respawns their sprite in the center of the grid space.

 

The game of Bomberman continues indefinitely, and the players’ running ranks are ordered by the number of points amassed, the highest scorer being in first place.
